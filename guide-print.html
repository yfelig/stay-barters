Phase 1
Finding the Right Property

Look for places that have something real but no web presence (or a bad one):

Good signs

  • Listed on Airbnb/Booking but no standalone website
  • Unique story or setting — farm, lake, jungle, family-run
  • Owner is hands-on and communicates personally
  • Reviews exist but guests have to dig to find them
  • Priced reasonably (not a 5-star resort — they already have agencies)

Red flags

  • Already has a good website → no trade value
  • Corporate-owned → no owner relationship
  • No personality / nothing to tell a story about
Phase 2
The Pitch

Keep it simple and human. This works best in person or over WhatsApp.

What to say

Script

"I build websites for places I love staying at. I'd like to trade — I'll build you a professional website that helps you get direct bookings (no Airbnb commission), in exchange for [X nights]. You keep everything — the code, the domain, all of it."

What you're offering

  • A 4-page website with their story, photos, facilities, reviews, and a direct WhatsApp booking link
  • A GitHub repo they own forever
  • A custom domain (first year covered, ~$10–20)
  • A handoff guide so they can update it themselves

Typical trade: 2–4 nights depending on property size. Agree upfront.

Phase 3
Preparation (Before Arrival)
  • Check if they have existing Airbnb/Booking/TripAdvisor — note reviews, star rating, review count
  • Note any social media (Instagram, Facebook)
  • Think about the domain name (e.g. aji-fruit-farm.lk, mamadwinery.com)
  • Bring: a camera or phone with a good lens, tripod if possible, a notebook
  • Clone or copy the template folder, name it after the property
Phase 4
The Interview

Do this on Day 1. Record audio if they're comfortable, or take notes. Ask in their language if possible — translate later.

Origin story

  • How long have you been running this place?
  • How did you come to own it / start this?
  • What was here before?
  • What changed along the way?

What makes it special

  • What do you want guests to feel when they arrive here?
  • What do guests say when they leave?
  • What can guests get here that they can't get anywhere else nearby?

The practical stuff

  • What's included in the price? (Breakfast? Transfers? Activities?)
  • What's check-in / check-out?
  • What activities do you offer on-site?
  • What's nearby that you can arrange? (safari, horse riding, day trips)
  • Do you have a pet / family members / staff involved?

Honest expectations

  • What should guests know before they arrive? (cold showers? no restaurant nearby? nature very close?)
  • Who is this place not for?

Booking logistics

  • What's your WhatsApp number?
  • What message do you want guests to send when they inquire?
  • Do you have a minimum stay?
  • Do you have seasonal pricing?

Reviews

  • Are you on Airbnb / Booking / TripAdvisor?
  • Can I use real quotes from your reviews on the website?
  • What's your star rating and approximate review count?

Social / contacts

  • Do you have Instagram or Facebook?
  • Do you want a contact email on the site?
Phase 5
Photography

Shoot across the stay, not all at once. Golden hour (6–8am, 4–6pm) is mandatory for outdoor shots.

Must-have shots

CategoryWhat to captureCount
Hero / establishingFull property from distance — golden hour, landscape in context2–3
The settingLake, garden, orchard, jungle, view. Mist, fruit, animals. Atmosphere.4–6
ExteriorBuilding from outside, entrance/door, veranda, path to the room3–4
Interior — roomFull room from doorway, the bed (clean linen), unique feature, seating area4–6
BathroomFull shot + feature shot if unusual (open-air, outdoor shower, vessel sink)2–3
FoodFull breakfast spread, close-up of local dish, fresh produce from the land4–6
The hostCandid portrait while doing something — tending farm, pouring tea, working2–3
ActivitiesKayaking, safari, hiking, pool — whatever's available. 1–2 per activity.3–6
DetailsFruit on tree, textures, morning light, architecture — fill grids with these5–10

Technical notes

  • Landscape (horizontal) for hero images, split sections, and photo grids
  • Portrait (vertical) for bathroom shots and some room details
  • No harsh noon sun — shoot in shade or wait for golden hour
  • Don't over-edit — natural colors, just lift shadows slightly
  • Optimize to 200–400KB per image before uploading (use squoosh.app)

WhatsApp CTA — the most important element

Put this on every page: floating button, nav button, dark footer section. Pre-fill the message.

https://wa.me/[COUNTRY_CODE][NUMBER]?text=Hi%20[Name]%2C%20I%20found%20your%20farm%20online%20and%20I%27d%20love%20to%20book%20a%20stay.%20Could%20you%20tell%20me%20about%20availability%3F

  • Rotate on dot click with opacity fade
Phase 7
Domain & Deployment

GitHub Pages setup

  1. Create a public repo: [property-slug] (e.g. ajis-mango-farm)
  2. Push all files to the main branch
  3. Go to repo Settings → Pages → Source: main / / (root) → Save
  4. GitHub gives you: [username].github.io/[repo-name]

Custom domain — DNS records

Add these 4 A records + 1 CNAME in the registrar's DNS settings:

Phase 8
Handoff

The owner gets everything. Walk them through it in person or on a call.

  • GitHub repo URL (public — they can clone or fork)
  • OWNER-GUIDE.md — plain-English instructions for updating text and photos
  • Domain credentials (transfer to their registrar account if bought for them)
  • WhatsApp number confirmed working in all booking CTAs
  • HTTPS confirmed active
  • Owner added as GitHub collaborator with push access

README to write in the repo: What the site is, the page structure, photo inventory (which photo is in which slot), how to update, Claude Code instructions for future AI-assisted edits.

From Stay #1
Lessons from Aji Fruit Farm
What we learned
  • Get the real story. The most compelling copy comes directly from the owner's words — especially the crisis/turning-point chapter. Don't write generic "welcome to our beautiful property" copy.
  • Cold showers = a feature, not a bug. Honest expectations build trust. Put the disclaimer directly on the rooms page — it pre-qualifies visitors and reduces disappointment.
  • WhatsApp pre-fills convert. The booking message should be pre-written and URL-encoded. Visitors send it with one tap.
  • 4 pages beat 1 page. The 4-page model lets each page tell a complete story. The story page drives emotional connection. Guests who read it become bookers.
  • Never use close-up abstract shots as teaser images. Every photo in a grid must answer one of: What does the setting look like? What is the room like? What makes it unique? A blurry door handle answers none of these.
  • Review quotes need to be real. Copy exact quotes from Airbnb/TripAdvisor. Attribution ("— Momoyo, Japan") makes them credible.
  • Roles stay clean. Photographer doesn't code. Builder doesn't art-direct the shots.
