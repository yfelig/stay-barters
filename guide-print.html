The Playbook — Part 1

The Steps

#PhaseWhen
1Find a place with a real story but no websiteBefore booking
2Pitch the trade — stay for a websiteWhatsApp or in-person
3Interview the owner, photograph the propertyDay 1–2 of stay
4Build the site with Claude Code (vibe code it)During or after stay
5Buy domain, deploy to GitHub Pages, handoffWithin a week

Good candidates: On Airbnb but no standalone site. Unique story — farm, lake, family-run. Owner communicates personally. Not a resort.

The Pitch

"I build websites for places I love. I'd like to trade — I'll build you a professional website that gets you direct bookings (no Airbnb commission), in exchange for [X nights]. You keep everything: the code, the domain, all of it."

What they get: 4-page site · GitHub repo they own · custom domain (first year on you, ~$10–20) · handoff guide so they can update it themselves.

Interview Questions

Origin story

  • How did you come to build this?
  • What was here before?
  • What changed along the way?

What makes it special

  • What do guests say when they leave?
  • What can't they get anywhere else nearby?
  • What do you want them to feel on arrival?

Honest expectations

  • What should guests know before arriving?
  • Who is this place not for?

Practical info

  • What's included? (breakfast, transfers?)
  • Check-in / check-out times?
  • Activities on-site + nearby?

Booking

  • WhatsApp number (international format)?
  • Preferred booking message?
  • Minimum stay? Seasonal pricing?

Reviews + social

  • On Airbnb/TripAdvisor? Star rating?
  • Can I use quotes from your reviews?
  • Instagram or Facebook?
The Playbook — Part 2

Photography

Shoot across the stay, not all at once. Golden hour (6–8am, 4–6pm) is mandatory for outdoor shots.

Must-haves

  • Hero — full property, distance, golden hour
  • Setting — lake, garden, nature, atmosphere
  • Exterior — building + entrance + veranda
  • Room — from doorway (zoomed out), bed, unique feature
  • Bathroom — full shot + feature if unusual

Story shots

  • Host — candid, doing something, not posed
  • Food — full breakfast spread + close-up of local dish
  • Activities — kayak, safari, bikes (1–2 per activity)
  • Details — fruit on tree, textures, morning light

Technical

  • Landscape for heroes + grids
  • Portrait for bathroom + room detail
  • No harsh noon sun
  • Natural colors — just lift shadows
  • Optimize to 200–400KB (squoosh.app)

The Playbook — Part 3

Claude Prompts

Use Claude Code to vibe code the site. These prompts build the full thing from scratch.

1. Kick off the build

Build a 4-page static website for [PLACE NAME] — a [brief description, e.g. "fruit farm with lakeside cottages in Sri Lanka"]. Stack: pure HTML/CSS/vanilla JS, no framework, no build step. Fonts: Cormorant Garamond (display) + Inter (body) via Google Fonts. Colors: warm linen background #f0ede4, deep [green/terracotta/navy] #1b3826 for CTAs and footer, near-black #18180f text. Pages: index.html (homepage), rooms.html (accommodation), place.html (the setting), story.html (owner story). WhatsApp number: [NUMBER in international format, no +] Booking message: "Hi [Name], I found your place online and I'd love to book a stay. Could you tell me about availability?" Put a floating WhatsApp button (fixed bottom-right) and a "Book Direct" nav button on every page.

2. Fill in the content

Here's the content for each section. Fill in all placeholders: Owner: [NAME]. Story: [paste interview notes — origin, crisis, turning point, now]. What makes it special: [key quotes from owner]. What's included: [list]. Honest note for rooms page: "[e.g. Cold water showers. Nature is very close. Not a resort.]" Reviews: [paste 5–6 real quotes from Airbnb/TripAdvisor with author name and country]. Location for map embed: [address or coordinates]. Activities nearby: [list with distances].

3. Place the photos

Photos are in /photos/. Place them as follows: - Hero image: [filename] - Homepage welcome section: [filename] - Host photo: [filename] - Rooms teaser main (veranda/exterior): [filename] - Rooms teaser side top (bed): [filename] - Rooms teaser side bottom (bathroom): [filename] - Room slider: [list 8–12 filenames] - Food carousel: [list 4–6 filenames] Add loading="lazy" to all images. Optimize with background: #e8e2d6 as placeholder.

4. Deploy

Create a CNAME file with one line: [domain.com] Push to GitHub. Repo name: [property-slug]. Enable GitHub Pages: Settings → Pages → main / root. Then walk me through the DNS records to add at [registrar name].

Tips

What actually matters
  • Get the real story. Owner's own words — especially the crisis/turning-point chapter — is your best copy. Never write generic "welcome to our beautiful property."
  • WhatsApp pre-fills convert. Write the booking message for them. URL-encode it. One tap to inquire. Put it everywhere: floating button, nav, dark footer on every page.
  • Honest expectations build trust. Put the "cold showers / no restaurant / nature is close" note directly on the rooms page. It pre-qualifies and reduces disappointment.
  • Story page is the secret weapon. Before → crisis → now. Guests who read it become bookers. Don't skip it.
  • Every teaser photo must answer a real question. "What does the room look like? What's the setting?" A close-up of a door handle answers nothing. Use exterior shots, beds, landscape.
  • Country-code domains add credibility. .lk, .co.il, .co.ke — cheap and local. Check the local registrar.
  • Reviews need attribution. "— Momoyo, Japan" makes a quote credible. "— Guest" does not.
